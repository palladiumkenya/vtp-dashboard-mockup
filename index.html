TRIPLE TESTING

Vertical Transmission Program and Triple Testing monitoring for HIV, syphilis, and hepatitis B across ANC, maternity, PNC, maternal treatment, infant prophylaxis, EID, and viral load outcomes.

Definition of terms

  • Triple Testing: HIV, syphilis, and hepatitis B screening within ANC workflows.
  • Positive outcomes: HIV, syphilis, and hepatitis B positive results among clients screened.
  • VTP treatment: Maternal and infant treatment and prophylaxis outcomes after positive diagnosis.
  • Infant Prophylaxis: Coverage of low-risk and high-risk infant prophylaxis interventions, including hepatitis B vaccination and immunoglobulin.

EARLY INFANT DIAGNOSIS

Definition of terms

  • Early Infant Diagnosis: Follow-up and HIV testing pathway for HIV-exposed infants (HEIs) from birth to final outcome at 18-24 months.
  • Total HEIs: Total number of HIV-exposed infants enrolled in care.
  • Total Paired HEIs: Number of HEIs with linked mother-baby records.
  • Pairing Gap: Number of HEIs not yet linked to maternal records.
  • Pairing Uptake: Proportion of HEIs that are successfully paired to maternal records.
  • EID Testing Cascade: Sequential infant HIV tests by age point (0-2 weeks, 6-8 weeks, 6 months, 12 months, 18-24 months).
  • Final Outcomes: Documented infant outcomes at end of follow-up (Negative, Positive, Died, LTFU, Undocumented).
  • Infant prophylaxis: Number of HIV-exposed infants started on infant prophylaxis.

Pairing Uptake

Mother-baby pairing tracks linked maternal and infant records through the Early Infant Diagnosis cascade, ensuring HEIs are followed from delivery through final outcome.

VIRAL LOAD

Definition of terms

  • Viral Load Uptake: Progress from currently on maternal HAART to eligible and then valid viral load results.
  • Viral Load Suppression: Distribution of valid viral load results into suppressed and unsuppressed outcomes.
  • Suppressed (<=50 copies/mL): Lower transmission risk category.
  • Unsuppressed (>50 copies/mL): Higher transmission risk category requiring enhanced adherence and clinical follow-up.
